# UX Flow

## Goal

The website should help a visitor understand the project quickly, calmly, and without fear.

The flow should answer:

1. What is this?
2. Is it peaceful?
3. What problem does it solve?
4. What values does it stand for?
5. What can I do?

## Visitor Journey

### Step 1 — First Impression

The visitor lands on the homepage.

They should immediately see:

- project name;
- short peaceful statement;
- no aggressive visuals;
- clear CTA.

Suggested hero text:

> Новый Мир — мирный проект о достоинстве, свободе передвижения, справедливости труда и глобальном сотрудничестве.

Primary CTA:

> Читать манифест

Secondary CTA:

> Понять проблему

### Step 2 — Safety Signal

Before heavy political or social ideas, the site should make clear:

- this is peaceful;
- this is not a cult;
- this is not a call to violence;
- this is not hatred toward nations or people.

Suggested block:

> Мы не строим культ личности и не призываем к насилию. Проект говорит о системных проблемах спокойно, открыто и мирно.

### Step 3 — Problem Explanation

The user sees a simple explanation of connected problems:

- poverty;
- borders;
- inequality;
- unfair labor;
- elite distance;
- restricted mobility;
- authoritarian control.

UX pattern:

- cards;
- short paragraphs;
- simple icons;
- optional connecting lines.

### Step 4 — Problem Map

The visitor sees how the problems are connected.

Example:

- Passport inequality limits movement.
- Limited movement reduces opportunity.
- Reduced opportunity keeps poverty alive.
- Poverty makes people easier to exploit.
- Exploitation strengthens elite distance.
- Elite distance protects unfair systems.

This section should feel like “now I see the structure”, not like propaganda.

### Step 5 — Manifesto Preview

Show a short version of the manifesto.

Goal:

- emotional clarity;
- human dignity;
- peaceful framing.

CTA:

> Читать полный манифест

### Step 6 — Principles

Show core principles as clear cards:

1. Peace
2. Dignity
3. Freedom of movement
4. Fair labor
5. Equal basic opportunity
6. No cult of personality
7. Open dialogue
8. Global cooperation

### Step 7 — What Can I Do?

Give safe actions:

- read;
- share;
- translate;
- discuss peacefully;
- contribute code/design/text;
- improve accessibility;
- suggest edits;
- help make the idea clearer.

Avoid asking users to do risky or illegal things.

### Step 8 — FAQ

The FAQ should reduce fear.

Important questions:

#### Is this communism?

Not exactly. The project is not trying to revive a fixed 20th-century ideology. It focuses on dignity, mobility, fair labor, and global cooperation.

#### Is this a political party?

No. It is a civic and philosophical web project.

#### Is this a revolution?

It is a peaceful project for rethinking systems, building tools, and spreading ideas without violence.

#### Who is the leader?

There should be no leader cult. The idea must be more important than any person.

#### Is this against countries?

No. It is against systems that make human dignity depend on birthplace, wealth, or passport.

## Mobile UX

Mobile is the priority.

Rules:

avoid long walls of text;
use collapsible FAQ;
make cards stack vertically;
keep CTAs visible;
use comfortable line height;
avoid tiny typography.

## Visual Mood

The project should feel:

calm;
serious;
humane;
modern;
open;
trustworthy.

Avoid:

militaristic visuals;
aggressive red/black propaganda style;
leader imagery;
flags as the core identity;
dystopian darkness;
chaotic animations.

## Conversion Goal

The first version should not pressure users.

Desired actions:

Read the manifesto.
Understand the problem.
Share the site.
Contribute peacefully.
Return later.
