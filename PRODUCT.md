# PRODUCT.md

## Product Name

Новый Мир

## One-line Description

A peaceful web project about dignity, freedom of movement, fair labor, and global cooperation beyond artificial divisions.

## Product Goal

Help people understand that many global problems are connected:

- poverty;
- borders and movement restrictions;
- inequality of opportunity;
- exploitation of labor;
- authoritarian systems;
- distance between elites and ordinary people;
- fear-based politics;
- inherited privilege.

The product should not simply complain. It should help people see the system clearly and imagine a more humane alternative.

## Target Audience

The project is for people who feel that the current world order is unfair, but do not want violence, hatred, or another authoritarian ideology.

Possible audience:

- young people tired of political cynicism;
- workers who feel invisible;
- migrants and people limited by passports;
- people from poorer regions;
- anti-war citizens;
- designers, developers, writers, and organizers;
- people who want a peaceful global future.

## User Feeling

The user should feel:

- “I am not crazy for seeing these problems.”
- “This is serious, but not hateful.”
- “This is peaceful.”
- “This is bigger than one country.”
- “I can participate without becoming part of a cult.”
- “This explains something I have felt for years.”

## Main Message

The world should not be organized so that a person’s life is mostly determined by birthplace, passport, inherited wealth, or proximity to power.

A more humane world should guarantee dignity, movement, safety, education, healthcare, and fair participation in society.

## MVP Structure

### 1. Hero

Purpose: explain the idea in one screen.

Content:

- project name;
- short statement;
- peaceful positioning;
- primary CTA: “Read the manifesto”;
- secondary CTA: “Explore the problems”.

### 2. The Problem

Purpose: show that the issue is systemic.

Topics:

- inequality;
- borders;
- labor;
- poverty;
- political power;
- restricted mobility;
- inherited privilege.

### 3. Problem Map

Purpose: show how problems connect.

Example nodes:

- Passport inequality
- Poverty
- Exploited labor
- Elite distance
- Restricted movement
- Authoritarian control
- Unequal education
- Unequal healthcare
- War and militarization

### 4. Manifesto

Purpose: present the moral core.

Should be short, readable, and calm.

### 5. Principles

Purpose: make the project safe and understandable.

Principles:

- Peace
- Dignity
- Equality
- Movement
- Fair labor
- Cooperation
- Transparency
- No cult of personality

### 6. What Can I Do?

Purpose: give peaceful actions.

Examples:

- read and share;
- translate;
- discuss respectfully;
- contribute design/code/text;
- organize local conversations;
- support open knowledge;
- help improve the project.

### 7. FAQ

Purpose: reduce fear and misunderstanding.

Important questions:

- Is this communism?
- Is this socialism?
- Is this a political party?
- Is this against countries?
- Is this calling for revolution?
- Who is the leader?
- How can I help?

## Content Rules

Do:

- explain;
- humanize;
- simplify;
- stay peaceful;
- invite dialogue;
- be honest about complexity.

Do not:

- threaten;
- insult groups of people;
- promise utopia;
- claim one ideology solves everything;
- build a personality cult;
- use manipulative urgency.

## Visual Direction

Suggested style:

- calm dark or light background;
- soft gradients;
- large readable typography;
- rounded cards;
- subtle motion;
- no aggressive symbols;
- no propaganda poster aesthetic.

Keywords:

- humane;
- planetary;
- soft;
- clear;
- serious;
- hopeful;
- mature.

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if a new visitor can answer:

1. What is this project about?
2. Is it peaceful?
3. What problems does it address?
4. What values does it stand for?
5. How can I participate?
